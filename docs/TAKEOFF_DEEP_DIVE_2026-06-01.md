# Sitelayer Takeoff / Blueprint / Canvas Subsystem — Deep Dive

**Date:** 2026-06-01
**Scope:** Blueprint document handling, drawing/measuring canvas (desktop + mobile), scale calibration & geometry math, assembly/formula expansion, AI auto-takeoff capture pipelines, and PDF/revision management — benchmarked against PlanSwift, the cloud/AI takeoff market, and the canonical takeoff/estimating domain model.
**Audience:** Lead architect + product. Goal: land real cladding/siding/framing subcontractor bids.
**Roadmap → workstreams:** the §6 roadmap below is mapped onto the independent-workstream seams in [`docs/PROJECT_DECOMPOSITION_PLAN.md`](./PROJECT_DECOMPOSITION_PLAN.md) §3.5 — which slice/owner builds each item, plus the sequencing couplings (pitch rides the `geometry.ts` split; the Condition layer spans seams).

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Sitelayer already has a **genuinely strong takeoff core** — and in several specific places it is _more correct_ than PlanSwift's 32-bit incumbent engine. The schema is forward-only across 10+ migrations (033–037, 108–110, 122–123); geometry is stored in clean board-space (0–100) with **anisotropic per-axis world scale** (`world_per_board_x/y`); a real hardened formula engine (expr-eval, sandboxed, 500-char cap) drives a multi-component assembly explosion with a transparent waste/burden/markup/profit pipeline; deductions use an elegant sign-separated `is_deduction` flag; and a real assembly-builder UI ships (`apps/web/src/screens/desktop/est-assemblies.tsx`, ~899 lines — the "no assembly UI" claim in older internal notes is **stale**). The product is cloud-native, mobile-aware, and offline-tolerant (optimistic concurrency / 409 retry), which structurally beats PlanSwift's desktop-only, workstation-locked, crash-prone reality.

**But the subsystem is missing the two abstractions that make a takeoff tool into an _estimating_ tool, and the one feature the market screams for:**

1. **No `Condition`** — the keystone reusable typed template that PlanSwift/On-Screen Takeoff build everything on. Sitelayer is _shape-first_ (draw a polygon, then post-hoc attach scope/assembly) instead of _condition-first_ (pick a typed template, draw against it). This is why takeoffs are not revision-proof or reusable across repeated elements.
2. **No pitch/slope math** — confirmed: zero `slope`/`pitch`/`secant` references in `packages/domain/src/index.ts`. For a cladding/siding/framing target this materially **under-quantifies** every gable, mansard, sloped soffit, and roof-adjacent surface.
3. **The flagship AI auto-takeoff is a UI dead-end** — every Run button hardcodes `dryRun: true` (`est-ai-takeoff.tsx:133`, `est-ai-count.tsx:116`), so even a correctly env-configured live deployment returns ~50 hardcoded stub quantities. The live Claude-vision path exists but no shipped screen feeds it a multipart PDF.

**Headline verdict:** Sitelayer is roughly at **"better-architected PlanSwift core, minus Conditions, minus pitch, minus a wired AI path, minus revision overlay."** The measurement/assembly/pricing plumbing is sound and in places exemplary. The gap to a market-winning cladding/framing product is **four well-scoped features** (Condition layer, pitch, wired live AI, revision overlay) plus a handful of cheap UX fixes (recompute-staleness signal, batch scale apply, assembly clone). None require a rewrite; the data model is a good foundation. The single most dangerous current state is the AI dead-end **silently fabricating numbers** an estimator could submit in a bid.

## 2. How PlanSwift & The Market Work (Canonical Workflow + AI Trend)

### 2.1 The three generations of takeoff/estimating software

- **Legacy desktop incumbents** — PlanSwift and On-Screen Takeoff/QuickBid (both ConstructConnect), plus Bluebeam Revu (PDF markup + measurement, no integrated estimating). Own the installed base; desktop-locked, workstation-bound, slow-evolving, criticized for crashes on large plan sets and weak support. PlanSwift's 2025 licensing change revoked perpetual licenses and forced ~$1,595–$1,749/user/yr, actively driving churn. (Sources: planswift.com/planswift-features, capterra.com/p/70808/PlanSwift, downtobid.com/blog/planswift-reviews.)
- **Cloud-native suites** — STACK and Procore Estimating moved takeoff + assemblies + cost data to the browser with real-time multi-user collaboration. STACK is the ease-of-use benchmark (now with STACK Assist AI auto-count + a limited mobile app); Procore wins on ecosystem (budget, QuickBooks, 3D auto-mapping). (stackct.com/takeoff-and-estimating, procore.com/estimating.)
- **AI auto-takeoff challengers** — Togal.AI, Kreo, Beam AI, plus AI bolted onto incumbents (On-Screen "Takeoff Boost", Trimble "Automate Takeoff" for MEP counts, eTakeoff SnapAI = Togal). Independent testing (Robotics & Automation News, Feb 2026; peer-reviewed Togal vs OST) converges on: **AI now automates ~85–95% of quantity extraction on clean architectural/standard commercial sheets at ~3–5% error and ~70–90% time savings**, but degrades on dense annotated commercial sets, MEP/plumbing symbols, hand-sketched and heavy-civil plans. The durable design pattern is **"AI-first draft + human verify"** with confidence-scored output and one-click override — _not_ black-box automation, which estimators distrust.

### 2.2 The canonical PlanSwift workflow (the model sitelayer is chasing)

1. **Create a Job**; import plan pages (PDF/image), drag-and-drop.
2. **Set scale per page** — pick units, calibrate from a known dimension or by drawing a line between two known points. **One scale per page** (multiple scales on a detail sheet require cropping/duplication — a documented limitation). Recommends scaling both H and V to catch directional stretch in scanned PDFs.
3. **Draw takeoff** with Linear / Area / Count / Segment tools, organized Job → Section → Item.
4. **Apply deductions/cutouts** to subtract openings for net quantities.
5. **Reuse typicals** with Continue-With and Advanced Copy Pro (Click-and-Copy, Copy-Offset with mirror/rotate, Array).
6. **Drag Parts & Assemblies** from the Templates pane onto a takeoff item; a variables dialog (wall height, stud spacing, sheet size, waste %) auto-expands one measurement into a full BOM + labor hours.
7. **Review** aggregated material/labor/equipment/cost on the Estimating tab.
8. **Output** via live two-way Excel link, Export-to-Excel-by-Page, and 13 built-in + custom reports.

### 2.3 The correct conceptual domain model (the four layers immature tools conflate)

1. **MEASUREMENT** — typed geometry primitives (linear, area, count, volume, **pitched/surface-area**), each living on a sheet that carries its own **scale**.
2. **CONDITION** — a reusable, typed, named/colored template that fixes the measurement type + drivers (height, thickness, slope/pitch, sides) and can **emit up to ~3 results from one drawn object** (e.g. one linear wall yields LF, single-side SF = H×L, both-side SF = H×L×2, CY = H×Th×L/27). The Condition is _the unit of reuse_ — what makes takeoffs maintainable across revisions.
3. **ASSEMBLY / RECIPE** — a formula graph that **explodes** a measured quantity into priced line items (material PARTS + LABOR + SUB), via **parent→child quantity propagation** (child qty references the parent's `[..\Qty]`, `[..\Height]`, `[..\Width]`). Coverage rate, waste factor, and UoM conversion live here.
4. **PRICING/COSTING** — line items priced from a cost DB (bare material + bare labor via crew daily-output + equipment, then O&P), rolled up by cost code, tracked across **three distinct snapshots: BID vs BUDGET vs ACTUALS** over one shared cost-code taxonomy, closing the bid→build→measure→adjust loop.

**Cross-cutting rigor the model demands:** trade-dependent deductions (drywall/paint/siding deduct openings; concrete does not, and may _add_ formwork); roof pitch slope factor = √(rise²+12²)/12 (6:12 = 1.118); known-dimension scale calibration per-sheet (PDFs print off-scale); immutable-baseline + overlay-diff revisions; strict UoM typing with explicit conversions (SY=9SF, CY=27CF, square=100SF). (Sources: help.constructconnect.com OST condition docs, rsmeans.com unit-assembly, planswift.com/blog/use-parts-assemblies, prebuiltml.com pitch-adjusted-measurements, knowify.com/resources/bid-build-measure-adjust.)

Notable correct choices: geometry quantity is **always stored positive**, sign carried by `is_deduction`; `world_per_board_x/y` stamped on every geometry so quantities recompute deterministically; soft-delete (`deleted_at`) throughout; `estimate_lines` frozen at recompute time (no FK back to component — immutable audit). 6 cladding assemblies seeded for the LA pilot (`110`).

### 3.2 Canvas tools (two implementations, shared data layer)

- **Desktop** `apps/web/src/screens/desktop/est-canvas.tsx` (~2987 lines) — full-bleed floating-palette. Tools: polygon, rect, lineal, arc (3-point tessellated to polyline), count. (`segment` is in the `Tool` union but **unwired** — no save/render branch.) Modes: draw / scale / select / ai-count / ai-takeoff. Wheel-zoom (cursor-anchored, 0.4×–12×), Space-drag pan, vertex snap (1.4 board units) + 6° ortho-lock, undo/redo per-vertex, deduction mode (polygon/rect only), marquee bulk-select (fully-enclosed only), vertex-drag geometry editing with live re-price, cross-sheet callout markers (hardcoded `SHEET_CALLOUTS` demo).
- **Mobile** `apps/web/src/screens/projects/takeoff-canvas.tsx` (~1673 lines) — responsive SVG, multi-draft picker, 7-tag elevation (`measurement.elevation`), AI quantity review (AgentSurface/Spark cards, confidence buckets high≥0.85 / 0.6–0.85 / <0.6, **ordinal never %**), category running totals.
- Shared geometry helpers: `packages/domain/src/index.ts` (`calculatePolygonArea[Scaled]`, `calculateLinealLength[Scaled]`, `calculatePolygonCentroid`).

### 3.3 Scale & geometry math

`apps/web/src/lib/takeoff/world-scale.ts` solves anisotropic `wx/wy` from page aspect + diagonal calibration vector: `wy = worldDist / √((Δbx·aspect)² + Δby²)`, `wx = aspect·wy`. Shoelace area × wx·wy; lineal Σ√((Δx·wx)²+(Δy·wy)²) — both **correct under independent per-axis scaling**. `sheet-scale.ts` regex-detects architectural (`1/4"=1'-0"`) and engineering (`1"=20'`) notations but is **read-only** (does not set calibration). `arc.ts` tessellates 3-point arcs (24 segments, round-to-2-decimals). `geometry-3d.ts` builds a 3D preview with calibration or relative-scale fallback. Server recomputes quantity at save via `apps/api/src/routes/takeoff-write.ts::calculateGeometryQuantity`.

### 3.4 Assemblies & formulas

`packages/domain/src/assembly.ts::resolveAssembly` multiplies measurement × `quantity_per_unit` × (1 + waste%) × unit_cost, grouped by kind. `packages/formula-evaluator/` is a hardened expr-eval (no member access, no assignment, no fndef; 500-char cap; 1e9 magnitude cap; 45 test cases). `packages/domain/src/markup.ts::applyMarkup` lays waste/burden/markup/profit with a transparent `MarkupBreakdownRow[]`. `apps/api/src/assembly-explode.ts` hydrates → resolves formulas → applies markup → bakes provenance lines. **Assembly builder UI ships** at `est-assemblies.tsx` with real CRUD via `lib/api/assemblies.ts`.

### 3.5 AI auto-takeoff / capture pipelines

Four sources: blueprint vision (Claude Opus, gated `BLUEPRINT_VISION_MODE=live` + `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY`), Apple RoomPlan, drone (orthomosaic sidecar), photogrammetry. Unified `TakeoffResult` zod schema (`@sitelayer/capture-schema`). Review floor hardcoded 0.7. **Confirmed dead-end:** desktop/mobile Run buttons post `payload: { dryRun: true }` (`est-ai-takeoff.tsx:133`, `est-ai-count.tsx:116`); live path (`blueprint-vision.ts`) only fires on a streamed multipart PDF that no shipped screen sends. Symbol/sensitivity/sheet-scope controls are presentational; auto-count is whole-draft, not per-symbol; auto-detect-room has no endpoint.

### 3.6 PDF / blueprint management

Client-side **PDFium via @embedpdf** (`pdf-page-canvas.tsx`) — no pdfjs fallback (pdfjs 4/5 hang past page 1; see MEMORY note). Lazy-loaded WASM, scale clamped to 4096px max side, text-layer extraction, search, bookmark extraction. Server: multipart upload (busboy, 200MB cap), optional first-page rasterization (poppler `pdftoppm`). Per-page calibration + separate verification. **Revision diff:** schema (`blueprint_page_diffs`) exists and a client-side raster luminance diff (Rec. 601, threshold 28) ships, but **no API route serves stored diffs** (confirmed: no diff route in `apps/api/src/routes/`), no worker populates them, and `affected_measurement_ids` is never consumed.

## 6. Prioritized Roadmap (Tied to Landing Cladding/Framing Bids)

### P0 — Quick wins / safety (days, ship first)

1. **Kill the AI fabrication risk (C1).** At minimum, gate the Run buttons + show an explicit "demo data" badge so no estimator submits stub numbers in a bid. Then wire the multipart-PDF live path. _This is a trust/liability issue before it is a feature gap._
2. **Recompute-staleness banner (H4).** A derived flag + persistent "Estimate out of date" banner. Stops silently-outdated estimates from going out the door.
3. **Batch scale apply + accept-detected-ratio (H5 + ratio-wiring).** Turns 22 manual calibrations into one confirm. Direct attack on the ~15%-rework scale-error class.
4. **Assembly clone (L1)** and **destructive-action confirmation (L5).** Cheap, high-frequency friction removers on top of the shipped builder.

### P1 — Differentiators (the wedge into cladding/framing)

1. **Condition layer (H1 + H7 + M4).** The keystone. Unlocks reusable typed templates, condition-first drawing, multi-result derivation, the natural home for pitch and trade-aware deductions, and the anchor for revision stability. Single biggest modeling investment; everything else compounds on it.
2. **Pitch / slope math (H2).** Required for trustworthy gable/soffit/sloped cladding quantities. Small math change, large credibility return for the target trades. Build it _on_ the Condition (pitch as a driver).
3. **Plan-revision overlay + version-stamped estimates (H3).** The market's #1 unmet pain and the clearest churn-from-incumbents wedge. Schema already exists — wire the route, the worker, and the affected-measurements badge.
4. **Parent-driver + conditional formulas (M2).** Makes one wall measurement drive stud-count/plate-LF/sheet-count — the difference between a "measuring tool" and PlanSwift's "estimating tool" for framing and cladding.
5. **Copy/typical/array tools (H6).** Repeated cladding bays and typical floors are bid constantly; this is daily-driver speed.

### P2 — Longer bets

1. **Bid/Budget/Actuals as three snapshots + per-cost-code variance (§4 bid/budget/actuals).** Freeze a budget at award; roll actuals by the same taxonomy; feed productivity back into assembly labor. Closes the bid→build→measure→adjust loop the benchmark calls the moat. Higher value once a few jobs are awarded and field data exists.
2. **Typed UoM + conversion layer (§4 units).** Eliminates a top silent-error class; needs care to migrate free-text data.
3. **Shared regional cost library + Excel price-book import (M5).** Removes hand-keyed unit costs; PlanSwift Import-from-Excel parity.
4. **Per-symbol auto-count endpoint + honoring scope controls (M1), multi-draft merge (M6), multi-scale detail regions (M3).**
5. **Cache-drift fixes (M7), pref-per-user (L4), segment/volume (L3), report breadth (L2), count UX (L6).**

## 7. Open Questions / Risks

1. **AI liability (C1).** Until the live path is wired _and_ clearly badged, the product can hand an estimator fabricated quantities. This needs a product/legal decision on whether to ship the AI surfaces at all in their current dead-end state. **Highest risk on the board.**
2. **Condition migration path.** Introducing `condition_id` over an existing tag-based model means a backfill: do existing measurements get auto-grouped into synthetic conditions, or is the Condition layer additive (tags remain as the legacy fallback)? The latter is safer but leaves two models coexisting.
3. **Pitch placement.** Pitch belongs on the Condition (P1.1), but shipping pitch _before_ the Condition layer (to unblock cladding bids sooner) means a temporary `pitch` column on measurements that later migrates onto the Condition. Acceptable, but a known double-handle.
4. **Budget snapshot semantics.** "Award" is not currently a hard state transition. Where does the freeze trigger live (change-order acceptance? a new explicit award action?), and how do change orders amend an immutable budget without mutating it?
5. **Live AI cost/scope for the LA pilot.** Blueprint vision is synchronous (Claude Opus blocks the HTTP handler on multi-page PDFs); production use likely needs a job queue + async callback, plus an entitlement/quota guard so the pilot doesn't run unbounded Anthropic spend.
6. **UoM migration.** Free-text units are pervasive; a typed UoM with rejection-on-incompatible-pairs could break existing rows mid-flight. Needs an expand/backfill/contract rollout (tolerate old + new during migration).
7. **Revision overlay accuracy.** The client raster luminance diff (threshold 28, no morphology) is advisory-grade and prone to antialiasing false positives. Server-side or vision-based diffing is needed before "X measurements affected" can be trusted enough to drive re-measure-only-deltas.
